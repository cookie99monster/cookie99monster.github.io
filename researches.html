Formula SAE suspension design and fabrication: An efficient approach

The design and fabrication of the suspension system of a Formula SAE vehicle are studied in this research paper. Data from Hertz Electric-DTU E21 Prototype was used that contains several vehicle, sensor, and track parameters to compute the forces in the suspension arms efficiently, using a spatial force model. Further, the CAD design was prepared, and optimization was done for the suspension members. The findings of this study are published, under the guidance of Prof. (Dr.) Atul Kumar Agrawal, in Springer Journal Recent Advances in Mechanical Engineering, a part of Lecture Notes in Mechanical Engineering (LNME) in 2021.

Crystallographic defect prediction in Si substrate during ion implantation: A research study utilizing ensemble learning

Ion implantation is a low-temperature process by which ions of one element are accelerated into a solid target, thereby changing the physical, chemical, or electrical properties of the target. In this research work, Si substrates are studied under varying implantation energies (from 100 KeV to 25KeV) using Boron (B) and Phosphorus (P) ions. Furthermore, their collision events have been captured, and an ensemble model using Random Forest and XGBoost has been trained on collision data to predict defects in the Si substrate. This research work has been carried out under the guidance of Dr. Sandeep Pal Chowdhury, Scientist-F at Solid State Physics Laboratory, a research facility under the Defence Research & Development Organization.
